What is Neurodevelopmental Screening?

Developmental screening helps assess your child's growth by using short tests or questionnaires. These tools, based on research, ask about your child's language, movement, thinking, behavior, and emotions. Your child should be screened if you or your doctor have a concern.However, developmental screening is a regular part of some of the well-child visits for all children even if there is not a known concern.

Neurodevelopmental screening tests are tools used to identify children who might have developmental delays, including Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD). These tests are not diagnostic but help determine if a child should be referred for a more comprehensive evaluation.

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) Screening Tests

1. Modified Checklist for Autism in Toddlers, Revised with Follow-Up (M-CHAT-R/F)
Age Range: 16-30 months
Description: A parent-completed questionnaire that identifies children at risk for ASD. It includes a follow-up interview to increase the accuracy of the screening.

2. Social Communication Questionnaire (SCQ)
Age Range: 4 years and older
Description: A parent-completed questionnaire that identifies children at risk for ASD. It includes a follow-up interview to increase the accuracy of the screening.

3. Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule (ADOS)
Age Range: 12 months to adulthood
Description: A standardized, observational assessment that involves a series of structured and semi-structured tasks.

4. Ages and Stages Questionnaires (ASQ)
Age Range: 1 month to 5½ years
Description: A series of questionnaires that screen communication, gross motor, fine motor, problem-solving, and personal-social skills.

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) Screening Tests

1. Vanderbilt ADHD Diagnostic Rating Scale
Age Range: 6-12 years
Description: A parent and teacher-completed questionnaire that evaluates ADHD symptoms and their severity, along with other behavioral concerns.

2. Conners' Rating Scales
Age Range: 6-18 years
Description: Parent, teacher, and self-report scales that assess ADHD symptoms and related behaviors.

3. Child Behavior Checklist (CBCL)
Age Range: 1½-18 years
Description: A comprehensive parent-report questionnaire that screens for a wide range of emotional and behavioral problems, including ADHD.

4. Swanson, Nolan, and Pelham Teacher and Parent Rating Scale (SNAP-IV)
Age Range: 6-18 years Description: A detailed questionnaire that assesses ADHD symptoms and severity.
